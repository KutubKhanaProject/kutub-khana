About The Maktaba Project

“My dear man, when Urdu Bazaar is no more, where is Urdu?” — Mirza Ghalib

Dehli’s Urdu Bazaar, the subject of this prophetic Ghalib quote, is breathing its final breaths. 40 years ago, the market boasted 80 Urdu bookstores but has barely 6 as of December 2024. Furthermore, shopkeepers who used to see long lines for new publications are now struggling to sell even a book a day. The publications have stopped almost completely. The decline of Dehli’s Urdu bazaar, as Mirza Ghalib foresaw nearly 200 years ago, is a symptom of a literary tradition in freefall.

Our Heritage

The Maktaba project borrows its name from the iconic Maktaba Jamia Limited, founded by Dr. Zakir Hussain in 1922. Once a leading publisher of Urdu literature (over 6000 books published), it closed its doors in September 2023 after years of neglect and a lack of funding.

The iconic Maktaba Jamia Limited in its final days.

Our Mission

We are two teenagers committed to preserving the literary tradition Maktaba Jamia once represented. We believe that Urdu literature’s best path to survival is to embrace the technological age, which provides a golden opportunity to store large amounts of knowledge with limited costs. With The Maktaba Project, we hope to create an open-source, free-of-cost library containing a comprehensive and easily navigable collection of Urdu books, regardless of genre or topic.

Our Vision

This website is to be a digital ebook library that allows users to easily read and/or download public domain texts in Urdu. We are particularly inspired by sites like the Gutenberg Project which make it easier for people to access literary texts free of charge.

“Knowledge is the bedrock of society”

These texts provide a variety of religious, social, and philosophical thought not found in any language. They describe emotions and cultural experiences that cannot be translated.

Help Us

We are always looking to expand our collection. Providing unique scans or even requesting the publication of your own original work will be of exceptional use to us in our mission. Furthermore, we can benefit greatly from work involving translation (into, or from Urdu). Sorting our exceptionally large collection can also help. Even ideas or requests can help us make this website better. We really mean it, we’re like two people right now and we need all the help we can get.
